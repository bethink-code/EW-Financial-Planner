Portfolio landing — three concept directions

Concept A — At a glance. The familiar category structure, elevated into a scannable snapshot: totals, premiums, household context up front. The attention layer runs in parallel as a collapsible strip and inline freshness flags. Lowest change cost; every existing mental model survives. Click any product row to open the Level 2 panel.

Concept B — Plan view. The portfolio organised around what the money is for, using the Product purpose data the platform already captures. The attention layer is woven into the goals themselves — a stale valuation isn't a separate to-do, it's a reliability warning on the Retirement goal. Click a goal to open the Level 2 panel.

Concept C — Command centre. Doesn't choose between lenses: plan strip on top, holdings below, and the attention queue as a persistent rail running alongside both. The most complete answer — and the densest. Click queue items to action them; resolving updates readiness live.
